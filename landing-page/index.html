The Problem

Following chats is tedious

Some teams coordinate investigations solely through chat channels. To stay up to date on large investigations, you have to read every chat message, though most messages are not relevant to you.

Investigations are tree-like in nature

A piece of malware may spawn a sweep, which may find another piece of malware, which may spawn another sweep, and so on.

But existing ticketing systems, like TheHive and JIRA, don't let you create subtickets of subtickets. Tickets in INCIDENTS can have any number of levels of subtickets.

Fear of missing things

Whether you're leading a large investigation or conducting a live response, there's many leads to follow. By explicitly marking tickets as leads in INCIDENTS, you can ensure you investigate each one.

Too much unneeded communication

Investigators spend a lot of time writing status reports, on update calls, and communicating with management.

Often, stakeholders just want a high-level overview of how an investigation is progressing. They can gain such an overview by watching an investigation's tree in INCIDENTS.
